## Not a substitute for professional judgement

Upholstery techniques, tools and materials carry real risks — sharp tools, staples, dust, adhesives and heavy frames among them. Follow the safety instructions for any tool or product you use, work within your ability, and if a job is beyond it, consult or hire a professional upholsterer. Any project you undertake is your responsibility and at your own risk.

## Fire safety and regulations

Where we discuss foam, fillings, fire grades (such as CMHR or Crib 5) and fire regulations, this is general guidance, not legal or compliance advice. Fire-safety law — including the UK Furniture and Furnishings (Fire) (Safety) Regulations — is detailed and changes over time, and stricter rules apply to furniture made or re-upholstered for sale, or for commercial and public settings. Always confirm the current regulations that apply to your work, and seek professional or legal advice for commercial projects.
